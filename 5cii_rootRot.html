Monstera adansonii

Oh no! Root rot

Excess water in the soil prevents the roots from taking in oxygen and they begin to decay. Signs of overwatering include leaf edema and yellow leaves. Root rot moves quickly and the plant is unable to support itself unless the plant is repotted and damage removed.

In mature plants, it’s easier to recover from underwatering than overwatering. Young propagations require moist but not soaked soil. This propagation didn’t take but now you know not to overwater future plants!
